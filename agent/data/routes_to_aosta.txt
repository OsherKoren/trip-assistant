OPTION 2A: Great St. Bernard Tunnel via Switzerland (Faster)
Route: A1 + A9 + Grand-Saint-Bernard Tunnel
Driving Time: ~3.5 hours
Total Time with Stops: ~4.5 hours
Tunnel Toll: ~30-50 EUR

Schedule:
12:00 PM - Depart GVA: A1 east to Lausanne (~45 min, 70 km), Lake Geneva views
12:50 PM - Stop 1: Martigny Castle (15 min)
  - Quick photo at medieval Chateau de la Batiaz overlook
  - St. Bernard intro nearby
1:30 PM - Route 9/21 to Tunnel (~1 hr, 50 km): Steady climb, tunnel crossing (10 min)
3:00 PM - Stop 2: Etroubles Village (20 min)
  - Charming Aosta Valley hamlet with frescoes and Roman ruins post-tunnel
3:45 PM - SS27 to Aosta (~45 min, 35 km): Valley descent
4:30-6:00 PM - Arrive Aosta (buffer for traffic/photos)

Notes: Safer/more direct via Martigny and the 5.8 km Great St. Bernard Tunnel.

OPTION 2B: Great St. Bernard Pass via Switzerland (Scenic)
Route: A1 + Route 21 over the pass
Driving Time: ~4.5 hours
Total Time with Stops: ~5.5 hours
Pass Status: Open mid-May to October (verify at alpen-paesse.ch)

Schedule:
12:00 PM - Depart GVA: Same A1 to Martigny (~1 hr, 80 km)
1:15 PM - Stop 1: Martigny Barryland (20 min)
  - St. Bernard dogs, demos (if time)
2:00 PM - Route 21 to Summit (~1.5 hrs, 45 km)
  - Climb hairpin bends to 2,469m pass
4:00 PM - Stop 2: Hospice Lake & Statue (20 min)
  - Glacial lake, St. Bernard monument, 360 deg Alps panorama
4:45 PM - SS27 Descent to Aosta (~1 hr, 50 km)
  - Epic valley views of Mont Blanc/Gran Paradiso
6:00 PM - Arrive Aosta (tight; skip one stop if needed)

Notes: Pass adds ~1 hr vs. tunnel but rewards with views/dogs. Pack water, check weather.
